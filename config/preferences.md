# Curation preferences

## Who I am
I am a founder and technical marketing consultant based in India, 
operating in the B2B SaaS and AI space. I am an active investor 
in Indian financial markets and follow international markets 
closely. I do not check my newsletter inbox daily — this digest 
is my primary mechanism for staying current. I want to be 
exceptionally well-read: not just across news, but across opinion, 
research, observations, and ideas from thought leaders across my 
fields. My goal is to read every item in the digest.

## What to select
Select between 8 and 12 articles per run. Never fewer than 8 on 
a normal day, never more than 12 even on a heavy content day.

Prioritise:
- High-impact developments: announcements, shifts, or ideas that 
  a well-informed person in my position must know about today
- Original thinking: opinion, analysis, research, and essays — 
  not just news aggregation. A thoughtful essay from a Substack 
  writer is as valuable as a breaking news story
- Discourse: what is the current conversation in my fields? What 
  are people debating, building, reacting to, or publishing that 
  is shaping the direction of these industries?
- India angle: always prefer a piece with an India-specific lens 
  over a purely international one when both cover the same topic

Never select:
- Press releases with no original analysis
- Duplicate coverage of the same story — pick the single best 
  version and discard the rest
- Listicles with no original thinking or perspective
- Crypto or Web3 content unless directly relevant to AI 
  infrastructure or Indian financial markets
- Promotional content or product marketing disguised as editorial

## Sections

### 1. AI
This is the highest priority section. Select 2–4 items per run.

Include everything across the AI landscape:
- Model releases, benchmarks, capability announcements
- Research papers and technical breakthroughs worth knowing about
- Enterprise AI adoption: how companies are deploying AI, 
  what is working, what is failing
- AI in workflows: how practitioners are implementing AI tools 
  in real work — engineering, marketing, sales, operations
- Strategic analysis: where is AI going, what does it mean for 
  industries, what are the risks and opportunities
- Opinions from founders, researchers, and practitioners — 
  not just news from journalists

### 2. Business & Technology
Select 2–3 items per run.

International and India business and technology news:
- Major company announcements, strategy shifts, M&A
- Startup ecosystem: funding rounds that signal trends, 
  founder stories, ecosystem developments
- India tech and business: Indian startups, Indian enterprise, 
  policy affecting Indian business, India-specific market moves
- Product launches and platform changes that matter at scale
- Opinion and analysis on where industries are heading
- B2B SaaS specifically: pricing models, GTM strategy, 
  category shifts, competitive dynamics

### 3. Financial Markets
Select 2–3 items per run. India first, then international.

Indian markets:
- Equity market movements and analysis
- RBI policy, SEBI regulations, government fiscal policy
- Startup funding and valuation trends in India
- Macro indicators: inflation, currency, GDP signals
- Sector-specific developments relevant to Indian investors

International markets:
- Fed decisions and US monetary policy
- Global macro: major economic shifts, trade policy, geopolitics 
  with market implications
- International market moves that affect Indian markets
- Currency and commodity moves

Always include pieces that connect Indian and international 
market dynamics — the two are deeply interconnected.

### 4. Economy & World
Select 1–2 items per run.

Major world events, geopolitics, trade, and economic policy 
that a well-informed global citizen should know about:
- India economic policy and governance
- US, China, EU policy with global implications
- Trade wars, sanctions, supply chain shifts
- Any major world development with downstream consequences 
  for business, markets, or society

### 5. Internet & Culture
Select 1–2 items per run when strong content exists. 
Skip this section entirely if nothing genuinely good is present.

Substantive pieces on:
- Internet culture and how digital life is evolving
- Social dynamics, relationships, and human behaviour 
  in the modern world
- Society and cultural shifts worth understanding
- Long reads or essays that a curious, well-read person 
  would find genuinely interesting

These should be pieces worth reading slowly, not just skimming.

## Selection rules
- Maximum 2 items from the same sender in a single digest
- On a heavy content day: apply stricter quality filters — 
  only include an item if it would genuinely change what 
  you know or think today
- On a slow day: include anything a well-read person would 
  regret missing, even if the bar is slightly lower
- Section headers are dynamic: skip a section if no strong 
  content exists for it in that run
- Sections must appear in priority order: AI first, 
  Internet & Culture last
- Every selected item must include the sender name and the 
  email subject line it came from — this is mandatory for 
  source traceability

## Synopsis style
Write like a trusted researcher briefing a busy founder who 
has 30 seconds per item. Be specific, direct, and opinionated 
about why something matters. Never be vague. Never summarise 
for the sake of it — every word should earn its place.
