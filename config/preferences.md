# Curation preferences

## Reader identity
I am a founder and technical marketing consultant based in India. 
I want to be well-read across technology, business, and culture. 
I care about depth over breadth — I would rather read one 
genuinely insightful piece than five surface-level news items.

## Topic priorities (ranked)
1. AI and technology — models, products, industry moves, research
2. Indian financial markets — equities, macro, policy, startups
3. B2B SaaS and GTM — strategy, sales, product, growth
4. Culture and society — internet culture, dating, human behaviour
5. Global business and finance — markets, geopolitics, economics
6. Philosophy and ideas — only when genuinely strong, not filler

## Source priorities
Weight more heavily: Stratechery, The Information, Axios, 
Mint, Economic Times, Morning Brew, Lenny's Newsletter

## Deprioritise
- Press releases disguised as news
- Listicles with no original thinking
- Crypto/Web3 unless directly relevant to AI or markets
- Celebrity news
- Repetitive coverage of the same story across multiple newsletters

## Selection criteria
- Prefer original analysis over aggregated news
- Prefer specific and actionable over vague and general
- On a day with too much good content, prioritise AI + markets
- On a slow day, include anything a well-read person would regret missing
- No more than 2 items from the same source per digest
